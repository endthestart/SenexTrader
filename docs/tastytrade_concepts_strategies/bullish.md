# Bullish

Source: https://www.tastylive.com/concepts-strategies/bullish

The content of the page "What Does Bullish Mean in Finance: Definition & Strategies | tastylive" describes the term "bullish" in the context of financial markets. It explains that "bullish" refers to a positive or optimistic outlook on the direction of an asset, market, or the overall economy, where prices or values are expected to rise. The term is believed to originate from the way a bull attacks, with an upward thrust, metaphorically applied to an upward movement in prices.

The article also differentiates between bullish and bearish sentiments, with bearish sentiment anticipating falling prices. Key characteristics of a bullish market include rising asset prices, positive investor sentiment, strong economic conditions, increased trading activity, and broad participation across sectors. It also provides advice on how to trade and invest in a bull market, emphasizing diversification, setting clear goals, and avoiding market timing. Several common bullish investing and trading strategies are listed, such as Covered Call, Naked Short Put, and Long Call Vertical Spread.
