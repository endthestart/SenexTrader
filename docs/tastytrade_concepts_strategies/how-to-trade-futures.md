# How to Trade Futures

Source: https://www.tastylive.com/concepts-strategies/how-to-trade-futures

The article "How to Trade Futures (Beginner's Guide)" from tastylive explains that futures are standardized contracts obligating the buyer and seller to trade an asset at a predetermined price and date in the future. These contracts are traded on exchanges and can cover various assets like commodities, stock indices, and currencies.

The article details how futures work, including the use of margin for leverage, and the two settlement methods: physical delivery or cash settlement. It outlines a six-step process for beginners to start trading futures:
1.  Open a Futures Trading Account.
2.  Formulate Your Market Assumption.
3.  Place Your Order.
4.  Monitor the Market and Manage Risk.
5.  Close Your Position.
6.  Review and Analyze.

Several beginner-friendly futures trading strategies are discussed, such as going long (buying with expectation of price rise), going short (selling with expectation of price decline), hedging (offsetting potential losses), and trend following. The article also lists various futures products available for trading, including commodities (crude oil, gold), stock indices (S&P 500, Nasdaq 100), currencies (Forex Futures), interest rates, metals, energy, and cryptocurrency futures.

Benefits of futures trading include leverage and the ability to hedge against price fluctuations. However, the risks involve amplified gains and losses due to leverage, market volatility, and liquidity risk. The futures market participants include hedgers (businesses, producers) and speculators (individual traders, institutional investors), with brokers and market makers facilitating trades.
