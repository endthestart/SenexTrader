# Fundamental Analysis

Source: https://www.tastylive.com/concepts-strategies/fundamental-analysis

The content of the URL https://www.tastylive.com/concepts-strategies/fundamental-analysis discusses fundamental analysis, defining it as a method to assess an asset's intrinsic value by examining economic, financial, and other qualitative and quantitative factors. The primary goal is to determine if an asset is overvalued or undervalued.

Fundamental analysis involves both qualitative and quantitative aspects. Qualitative analysis includes assessing management quality, brand strength, industry position, company culture, and the regulatory environment. Quantitative analysis focuses on financial statements, financial ratios (like P/E, ROE, Debt-to-Equity), earnings, dividends, and industry-specific metrics.

The article highlights several pros and cons of fundamental analysis. Advantages include an in-depth understanding of a company's health, a long-term perspective, informed decision-making, and risk management. Disadvantages involve it being time-consuming, subjective in nature (especially for qualitative factors), relying on historical data (lagging indicator), and potentially overlooking broader market trends due to concentrated emphasis.

When comparing fundamental analysis with technical analysis, the article states that fundamental analysis focuses on an asset's intrinsic value and "why" prices move, typically for medium to long-term investment horizons. Technical analysis, on the other hand, uses historical price and volume data to forecast future price movements, focusing on "what" and "when" for short to medium-term forecasts. Many investors use a hybrid approach combining both methods.
