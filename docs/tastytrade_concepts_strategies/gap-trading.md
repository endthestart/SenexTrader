# Gap Trading

Source: https://www.tastylive.com/concepts-strategies/gap-trading

The provided article from tastylive.com, titled "Gap Trading Strategies: How to Trade the Gap (Beginner's Guide)", explains gap trading as a strategy that capitalizes on price gaps in financial instruments. These gaps occur when an asset's opening price significantly differs from its previous day's closing price.

Key aspects of gap trading include:
*   **Identifying Gaps:** Traders use technical analysis to spot discrepancies between the previous day's closing price and the current day's opening price.
*   **Gap Classification:** Gaps are categorized into common gaps, breakaway gaps, and exhaustion gaps, each with different implications for trading.
    *   **Common Gaps:** Occur within a trading range and are less significant, often resulting from minor news or price fluctuations.
    *   **Breakaway Gaps:** Signal the start of a new trend after a period of consolidation, indicating a strong shift in market sentiment.
    *   **Exhaustion Gaps:** Appear at the end of a trend, suggesting a potential reversal as the prevailing trend loses momentum.
*   **How it Works:** Gap trading relies on the belief that these price differences offer profit opportunities. Traders select strategies based on the gap type and market conditions, employing risk management techniques like stop-loss orders.
*   **"Gap is Filled":** This term means the price of a financial instrument has returned to the level where the gap initially occurred, effectively closing it on the chart.

The article outlines several gap trading strategies:
*   **Gap Filling Strategy:** Assumes most gaps will eventually be filled, so traders take a position opposite to the gap's direction.
*   **Breakaway Gap Trading Strategy:** Involves taking positions in the direction of the gap, assuming the new trend will continue.
*   **Exhaustion Gap Trading Strategy:** Traders take positions contrary to the gap direction, anticipating a trend reversal.
*   **"Gap and Go" Strategy:** Focuses on trading gaps at market open that align with the prevailing trend, capitalizing on momentum.
*   **"Gap and Run" Strategy:** Involves trading gaps at market open with the expectation that the price will continue moving in the gap's direction.

The article emphasizes that gap trading involves risk and requires thorough development and testing of strategies, continuous monitoring, and adaptability to evolving market dynamics.
